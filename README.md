###HR Analytics Dashboard Explanation
This HR Analytics Dashboard provides key insights into employee attrition, satisfaction, and salary trends. It helps HR professionals identify workforce trends, understand attrition factors, and optimize retention strategies. The dashboard consists of several visualizations representing different aspects of employee attrition.

###Key Metrics Overview
Total Employees: 1,470

Total Attrition Cases: 237 (16.1% Attrition Rate)

Average Age: 37 years

Average Salary: $6,500

Average Tenure: 7 years

###Attrition by Age and Education
The highest attrition occurs in the 26-35 age group, with 116 employees leaving.

Employees with Life Sciences (38%) and Medical (27%) backgrounds contribute significantly to attrition.

###Attrition by Salary and Tenure
The majority of attrition (163 employees) comes from those earning up to $5K, indicating salary as a crucial factor.

The highest attrition occurs at 2-3 years of tenure (59 employees), suggesting that early career employees are more likely to leave.

###Job Satisfaction and Attrition by Role
Laboratory Technicians (62), Sales Executives (57), and Research Scientists (47) have the highest attrition rates.

Job satisfaction ratings indicate that most employees leaving had lower satisfaction scores.

###Conclusion
This dashboard highlights salary, tenure, and job role as significant attrition factors. Organizations should focus on improving compensation, job satisfaction, and career growth opportunities to enhance employee retention.
